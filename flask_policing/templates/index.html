What is SF Analytical Policing?

Analytical Policing for the San Francisco Bay Area provides law enforcement and the community with innovative methods of increasing awareness of daily crime occurrences while helping to decrease crime. Through a detailed database, law enforcement can extract crime trends in order to better predict and stop criminal activity. Our data is unique to San Francisco, and continues to grow with our authorized guest's crime reports. As individuals report crimes directly through our Analytical Policing program, we can monitor crimes that take place all throughout the San Francisco Bay Area. To report a crime, please go to Report a Crime. If you have any questions pertaining to Analytical Policing, please feel free to contact us as well!

What is Analytical Policing?

Analytical policing is an innovative technique used in law enforcement in order to anticipate incidents of crime. Through mathematical and statistical perspectives, Analytical policing is an effective and proven method to identify criminal activity, identities and tendencies of offenders, and that of their victims. In recent years, analytical policing has been successfully implemented into various departments across the United States. Along with its contributions in decreasing crime, analytical policing has been instrumental in creating stronger relationships between law enforcement and the community in stopping and resolving crime.

Features of Our Data

12 years of crimes recorded and detailed for the San Francisco Bay Area

Our data is enhanced to include every aspect of crime, from law enforcement demographics to suspect demographics, in addition to crime locations and times. Every detail contributes to a more accurate picture of the occurrences of crime committed in San Francisco, leading to a more successful and effective analytical policing strategy, and a safer community.

Behind the Data

The data set we have provided is based on real data managed by SFOpenData and hosted online by Kaggle. Additional details and data patterns have been injected, and are based off of real crime demographics. For instance, demographics of race, gender, and age refer to data collected by the FBI. Take a peek at the resources and statistics we referenced in building our data:

  • Offender demographics of race and gender were influenced by data collected by the FBI

Hispanics and Asians make up approximately 13% each, African Americans make up 10%, and other races, those including Native Americans and Asian or Pacific Islanders, make up the rest.
